Maps and directions

The University of Wolverhampton operates car parks across its main sites at City and Springfield Campus in Wolverhampton, Walsall Campus, and Telford Campus. Use our Maps and directions webpages to find the locations of our campuses, buildings and car parks.

City campus Wolverhampton

No fuss, however you get to us.


Bus:

Wolverhampton Bus Station: 6 minutes’ walk from campus, served by buses from across the region.



Train:

Wolverhampton Train Station: 7 minutes’ walk from campus. It’s on the main Birmingham, London, Manchester, Liverpool and Edinburgh lines.



Metro:

Wolverhampton St Georges metro stop (which goes to Birmingham Snow Hill via West Bromwich) is 7 minutes’ walk from campus. Services run every 6-8 minutes throughout the day. 



Driving:

Wolverhampton City Campus is based in the city centre of Wolverhampton.


The closest motorway junction is Junction 10 of the M6. City Campus is split into two sites: City Campus Molineux (signposted North on road signs) and City Campus Wulfruna (signposted South on road signs).

Both are signposted from the Ring Road and the A449.

The main entrance and registration for Open Day is on City Campus Wulfruna. Open Day Parking is on City Campus Molineux, with a short walk to the main entrance.



Parking:

Limited parking for Open Day visitors is available on City Campus Molineux (signposted North on road signs), opposite the Lord Swraj Paul Building (MU), on Molineux Street, labelled 'staff only'. Use WV1 1SG for satnavs.

There is very limited metered parking by the main reception on Wulfruna Street.

There are several metered, on-street, or pay and display car parks within short walking distance.

For locations of the nearest disabled parking bays, visit the Wolverhampton City Council pages.



Understanding room numbers at the University

The first letter indicates the campus (M=City, S=Telford, W=Walsall). The second letter indicates the building reference. The room numbers follow, with the first indicating the floor level that the room is located on (e.g. 001 would be on the ground floor; 101 on the first floor, etc.).

By road

Junction 10 of the M6 is the nearest motorway junction. The University is split into two sites - City Campus North and South - both of which are signposted from the Ring Road and the A449.

Although there is only very limited metered parking by the main reception on Wulfruna Street, there are a number of public pay car parks within walking distance.

Of these, the Civic Centre car park, at the end of Wulfruna Street, or the long-term car park off Whitmore/Broad Street are the closest.



Parking

General parking spaces are indicated on the Wolverhampton street map.
Details of the type of parking (public, disabled or staff) can be found on our interactive campus map.


Open Day Parking

If you are arriving by car, there is parking opposite the Lord Swraj Paul Building (MU). Car parks are labelled 'staff only' but are open for visitors to use on the day (see street view of our car parks). The postcode for Sat Nav is WV1 1SG. If these are full, then there is overflow parking available at the football club's Stan Cullis Car Park opposite Asda, on Jack Hayward Way (see interactive map). Once you have parked your car, facing the Business School building, turn right and walk up the road, through the underpass and up the stairs. Turn right at the top of the stairs and you will find the Ambika Paul entrance in the building on your left-hand side.



 public transport

By bus: Buses come into the city centre from all over Wolverhampton and the West Midlands.

By train: The train station is on the main London, Birmingham, Manchester, Liverpool and Edinburgh lines. The station is on Railway Drive, just outside the Ring Road.

By Metro: The Midland Metro offers a service every 10 minutes throughout the day from Birmingham Snow Hill to Wolverhampton St Georges terminus via West Bromwich.



On foot

The City Campus is right in the centre of Wolverhampton. It is a seven-minute walk from both the train station and St Georges Metro terminus, and a five-minute walk from the main bus station.



Directions

From the train station - as you exit walk straight ahead and veer right across the road bridge going over the ring road.
From the bus station - walk straight ahead as you exit, past WH Smiths on your left and continue to the crossroads.
At the cross roads continue straight ahead down Lichfield Street, you will see the Britannia hotel and The Grand Theatre on your left as you continue down the road.
At the next junction where you reach The Hooded Ram and the Royal London Buildings turn right – you will see The George in front of you. This is the beginning of the 'University Quarter'
From the Metro - upon exiting the Metro station, walk towards Primark, cross the road and then take a right alongside Marks and Spencers
Continue along this road - it will bend slightly to the right and then back to the left. Keep going until you reach a crossroads - you will have The Hooded Ram on your left.
Cross over and continue on the same direction around the Royal London Buildings. You should now see The George in front of you

Walsall campus


Bus

The number 51 bus from Walsall Bus Station takes approx. 12 minutes and stops on Jesson Road, near campus.



Train:

Walsall Train Station is about half an hour’s walk from campus. Taxis are available, or the bus station (see above) is a short walk away.



Driving:

Walsall Campus is a few minutes’ drive from Junction 9 of the M6 motorway. Take the A461 Bescott Road towards Walsall. Get into the right hand lane and follow the right fork of the A4148, signposted Birmingham.

You will pass The Performance Hub on the left. At the following traffic lights, turn left into the Performance Hub entrance (off The Broadway, A4148).

Use WS1 3EZ for satnavs.



Parking:

Limited parking for Open Day visitors is available, please use The Performance Hub entrance. Use WS1 3EZ for satnavs.

Disabled parking is available at the Gorway Road entrance.

Telford campus



Train:

Telford Central  station is 17 minutes’ walk to the campus. It is not on a direct bus route.



Driving:

Telford Campus is between junctions 4 and 5 of the M54.

Use TF2 9NN for satnavs.



Parking:

Open Day parking for visitors is available. Use TF2 9NN for satnavs.

Disabled parking is available.

Getting to Telford Campus

By road

Approaching from the east (inc. Birmingham and Wolverhampton), leave the M54 at Junction 4 and take the A4640 (previously the B5060) Castle Farm Way. At the first roundabout, turn left on to the A5 Telford Way (also known as St George's bypass), then at the Priorslee roundabout take the second exit on to Shifnal Road. The campus entrance is a short drive on the left-hand side.

Coming from the west (inc. Shrewsbury and Wales), leave the M54 at Junction 5 and take the A5 Rampart Way. Go straight over the interchange on to the A5 Telford Way, cross over the motorway, and at the Priorslee roundabout take the Shifnal Road turning.

Car parking is available on campus. View the interactive map for details.


By public transport

By train: You can get a direct train into Telford Central from Birmingham (30 to 35 minutes), Wolverhampton (15 to 25 minutes) and stations on the Shrewsbury line. From there, you can catch the or pick up a taxi to the campus (or even walk in about 15 minutes).

By bus: There is no direct bus service to the Campus.

Springfield campus, wolverhampton



Our Springfield Campus is a short walk from Wolverhampton City Centre, and from City Campus. Travelling in by public transport will bring you to Wolverhampton City Centre.

Wolverhampton Bus Station: 9 minutes’ walk from Springfield Campus, served by buses from across the region.  If travelling from the north, the closest bus stop may be on Cannock Road (Badger Drive) or Stafford Street (A449, stopping by The Food Warehouse/ Home Bargains).



Train:

Wolverhampton Train Station: 7 minutes’ walk from campus. It’s on the main Birmingham, London, Manchester, Liverpool and Edinburgh lines.



Metro:

Wolverhampton St Georges metro stop (which goes to Birmingham Snow Hill via West Bromwich) is 15 minutes’ walk from Springfield Campus. Services run every 6-8 minutes throughout the day. 



Driving:

Springfield Campus is just outside the main city centre of Wolverhampton.

The closest motorway junction is Junction 10 of the M6. City Campus is split into two sites: City Campus Molineux (signposted North on road signs) and City Campus Wulfruna (signposted South on road signs). Both are signposted from the Ring Road and the A449.

To drive to Springfield Campus, from the A449 Stafford Road section of the ring road, follow signs for the A460, Cannock Road. Go past the first right turn for Cambridge Street (passing Casper Polish supermarket), and take the second right, Springfield Road and immediate right onto Hilton Street, which merges into Culwell Street and leads to one-way access onto Grimstone Street, and the car park.

The postcode WV10 0JP will take you through to the car park which is on Grimstone Street.



Parking:

Limited parking for Open Day visitors is available at Springfield Campus - access from Grimstone Street.

Alternatively, there is more Open Day parking available on City Campus Molineux (signposted North on road signs), opposite the Lord Swraj Paul Building (MU), on Molineux Street, labelled 'staff only'. Use WV1 1SG for satnavs. 

For locations of the nearest on-street, or pay-and-display car parks, as well as disabled parking bays, visit the Wolverhampton City Council pages.

By road

Situated on Glaisher Drive, just off the Stafford Road (A449), the University of Wolverhampton  Science Park is five minutes from Wolverhampton city centre, the bus and railway stations. It is accessible from the M6 motorway, northbound and southbound. Parking is available on site.


By public transport

By bus: The 4 bus services run frequently from the city centre along the Stafford Road, with a stop just outside the entrance.

By train: Wolverhampton train station is the nearest station. The University of Wolverhampton  Science Park is just a short taxi ride, or 15-minute walk, away.
